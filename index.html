Biography

I am a PhD candidate specializing in Materials Science and Biomolecular Engineering at Georgia Institute of Technology. My research focuses on the discovery and design of functional oligonucleotides (aptamers) for diagnostic and therapeutic applications. With a passion for leveraging computational tools and molecular biology techniques, I aim to develop next-generation ligands with enhanced stability and performance.

Driven by a foundation in Mechanical Engineering from Cal Poly Pomona, my academic journey has been focused on applying engineering principles to solve complex biological problems. I am passionate about data analysis, automation, and structural modeling to inform rational aptamer design.

Research Interests

HT Ligand Screening

Utilizing SELEX and molecular evolution to screen diverse oligonucleotide libraries, leading to the discovery of novel ligands for specific protein targets.

Computational Aptamer Design

Applying structural modeling tools like AlphaFold and PyMOL with Python automation to evaluate aptamer performance and inform next-generation design.

NGS & Assay Development

Optimizing NGS workflows and developing qPCR/ddPCR assays to characterize aptamer-protein binding affinity and dissociation constants (Kd).
